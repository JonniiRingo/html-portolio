1. Introduction

Quantum field theory (QFT) provides a framework for particle interactions and force behaviors at quantum scales. This study aims to model quantum field desynchronization and its implications for advanced propulsion. The desynchronization concept is explored through computational models, potentially advancing high-energy applications.

2. Theoretical Framework

Quantum Field Equations

The scalar quantum field ϕ follows the Klein-Gordon equation:

\[\Box \phi + m^2 \phi = 0\]

where □ denotes the d'Alembertian operator. For a massless field, this reduces to:

\[\Box \phi = 0\]

Stress-Energy Tensor

The stress-energy tensor \(T_{\mu\nu}\) is given by:

\[T_{\mu\nu} = \partial_\mu \phi \partial_\nu \phi - \frac{1}{2} g_{\mu\nu} (\partial_\lambda \phi \partial^\lambda \phi + m^2 \phi^2)\]

For a massless field, this simplifies to:

\[T_{\mu\nu} = \partial_\mu \phi \partial_\nu \phi - \frac{1}{2} g_{\mu\nu} \partial_\lambda \phi \partial^\lambda \phi\]

3. Methods

Simulation Setup

Numerical simulations were conducted on a discretized grid to solve the Klein-Gordon equation, with high magnetic field influences included as potential terms. Parameters like grid size, time steps, and spatial intervals were optimized for stability.
